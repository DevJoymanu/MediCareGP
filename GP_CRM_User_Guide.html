Chapter 1
Getting Started

G.P CRM is a secure, web-based practice management system. It runs in your browser — no software installation is required. You can access it from your consulting room computer, a tablet, or any device with an internet connection.

Logging In
  1. 1
    Open your browser Navigate to your practice URL: https://medicareapp.up.railway.app
  2. 2
    Enter your credentials Type your username and password. These were set up when your account was created.
  3. 3
    Click "Sign In" You will be taken directly to your Dashboard.
⚠
Forgotten password? Contact your practice administrator to reset your password. Do not share your login credentials with anyone, including reception staff — each user should have their own account.
The Dashboard — Your Daily Overview

After logging in you land on the Dashboard. This gives you a real-time snapshot of your practice at a glance — no clicking required.

Dashboard PanelWhat it shows you
Today's AppointmentsEvery patient booked for today, in time order, with their reason for visiting.
Stat Cards (top row)Total patients on the system, today's appointment count, how many are still Scheduled, and pending tasks.
Pending TasksYour four highest-priority outstanding tasks — lab follow-ups, callbacks, referrals, etc.
Follow-ups DuePatients whose follow-up date is coming up soonest.
Recent PatientsThe last four patients registered, for quick access.
Week SummaryTotal appointments and consultations completed this week.
💡
Tick tasks directly from the Dashboard. Click the checkbox next to any pending task to mark it done without leaving the dashboard.
Navigating the System

The dark sidebar on the left is your main navigation. It stays visible on every page.

Sidebar ItemTakes you to
🏠 DashboardDaily overview and stats
👥 PatientsFull patient list and search
📅 AppointmentsSchedule and manage bookings
📋 ConsultationsSOAP notes and clinical records

📊 AnalyticsPractice statistics and trends

Red badge numbers on sidebar items (e.g. next to Tasks or Billing) indicate items needing attention.

Chapter 2
Patient Records

All clinical activity in G.P CRM is linked to a patient record. Registering a new patient takes about two minutes. Once registered, the patient's complete history — appointments, consultations, documents, vitals, and invoices — is available in one place.

Searching for a Patient
  1. 1
    Click Patients in the sidebar.
  2. 2
    Type in the search box — you can search by full name, phone number, ID number, medical aid name, or chronic condition.
  3. 3
    Click the patient's name in the results to open their full record.
Registering a New Patient
  1. 1
    Click Patients in the sidebar, then click the "+ New Patient" button.
  2. 2
    Complete the registration form. Required fields are marked Required.
  3. 3
    Click "Save Patient". A file number is automatically assigned.

Date of Birth RequiredAge is calculated automatically.
Gender RequiredMale / Female / Other.
ID Type & Number OptionalSouth African ID or Passport number.
File NumberAuto-generated — you cannot change this.
Phone & Alternate Phone OptionalMobile preferred for appointment reminders.
Email OptionalRequired if you want to email invoices to this patient.
Address OptionalResidential address for records.
Next of Kin OptionalName and contact number of nearest relative.
Medical Aid Scheme OptionalScheme name, plan, membership number, principal member.
Blood Type OptionalA+, A−, B+, B−, AB+, AB−, O+, O−.
Allergies OptionalList one per line, e.g. "Penicillin", "Sulfonamides".
Chronic Conditions OptionalList one per line, e.g. "Type 2 Diabetes", "Hypertension".
Current Medications OptionalFree text. One medication per line recommended.
Previous Surgeries OptionalFree text surgical history.
Family History OptionalRelevant hereditary conditions.
Smoking / Alcohol / Substances OptionalLifestyle history for clinical context.
POPIA & Treatment ConsentTick once the patient has signed consent forms.
The Patient Profile Page

Clicking any patient's name opens their full profile. From here you can see and access everything related to that patient:

📋 Clinical Summary
  • Demographics & contact info
  • Blood type, allergies, chronic conditions
  • Current medications
  • Lifestyle history
📅 Appointments tab
  • All past and upcoming appointments
  • Quick link to book a new appointment
📝 Consultations tab
  • Every SOAP note ever written for this patient
  • Quick link to start a new consultation
📄 Documents tab
  • Prescriptions, sick notes, referral letters
  • Uploaded files and attachments
Recording Vital Signs
  1. 1
    Open the patient's profile.
  2. 2
    Click "Add Vitals".
  3. 3
    Enter the readings. For blood pressure, type it as 120/80 — the system splits it automatically into systolic and diastolic.
  4. 4
    BMI is calculated automatically from weight and height.

Editing or Deleting a Patient Record

Open the patient's profile and click Edit to update any field. To delete a patient, click Delete — you will be asked to confirm. Note that deleting a patient permanently removes all their appointments, consultations, and documents.

🚨
Deletion is permanent. Always verify you have selected the correct patient before deleting. This action cannot be undone.
Chapter 3
Appointments

The Appointments module is your booking register. You can view the full schedule, filter by date or status, book new appointments, and update their outcome after the consultation.

Booking a New Appointment
  1. 1
    Click Appointments in the sidebar, then "+ New Appointment".
  2. 2
    Select the patient from the dropdown. If they are not yet registered, register them first (see Chapter 2).
  3. 3
    Choose the date and time.
  4. 4
    Enter the reason for visit (e.g. "Hypertension follow-up", "Acute chest pain").
  5. 5
    Click "Save Appointment".
💡
You can also book an appointment directly from a patient's profile page — the patient field will be pre-filled.
Appointment Statuses

Every appointment carries one of four statuses. Update the status after each patient encounter.

StatusWhen to use it
ScheduledBooked but the appointment has not happened yet. This is the default when you create a new appointment.
CompletedThe patient attended and was seen. Change to this after the consultation is done.
CancelledThe patient cancelled or you cancelled the slot. Use the Cancel button on the appointment.
No-ShowThe patient did not arrive and did not contact you.
Filtering the Appointment List

The Appointments page shows all appointments, newest first. Use the filter bar to narrow the list:

FilterWhat it does
Status filterShow only Scheduled, Completed, Cancelled, or No-Show appointments.
Date filterPick a specific date to see only that day's bookings (useful for a daily schedule view).
Date rangeSet a start and end date to view a week or month of appointments.
Optional Medical Aid Fields

When completing or editing an appointment, you can record the following medical aid billing details:

FieldPurpose
ICD-10 CodePrimary diagnosis code for this visit, for billing purposes.
Authorization NumberMedical aid pre-authorisation number if required for the procedure.
Referring DoctorName of the GP or specialist who referred the patient.
Chapter 4
Consultation Notes

Consultations are where you record your clinical findings using the standard SOAP format — Subjective, Objective, Assessment, and Plan. Every consultation is permanently stored and linked to the patient's record, giving you a complete longitudinal clinical history.

Creating a New Consultation Note
  1. 1
    Click Consultations in the sidebar, then "+ New Consultation".
    Or, open the patient's profile and click "New Consultation" from the Consultations tab — the patient field will be pre-filled.
  2. 2
    Select the patient and optionally link to an existing appointment.
  3. 3
    Fill in the SOAP fields (see below).
  4. 4
    Click "Save Consultation".
The SOAP Note Fields
SOAP FieldWhat to record here
Chief Complaint OptionalThe patient's presenting complaint in their own words. E.g. "Sore throat for 3 days".
S — Subjective RequiredHistory of the presenting illness. Symptoms, onset, duration, character, associated symptoms, relevant PMH.
O — Objective RequiredExamination findings. Vital signs, physical examination findings, investigation results.
A — Assessment RequiredYour diagnosis or differential diagnosis.
Differential Diagnosis OptionalOther diagnoses you are considering or ruling out.
P — Plan RequiredTreatment plan. Medications, investigations ordered, lifestyle advice, referrals.
ICD-10 Code OptionalBilling diagnosis code.
Referral To OptionalSpecialist or facility you are referring the patient to.
Referral Reason OptionalReason for referral, used on the referral letter.
Prescriptions OptionalMedications prescribed during this consultation.
Follow-up Date OptionalWhen the patient should return. Sets an automatic follow-up reminder on your Dashboard.
Sick Note Details

If you are issuing a sick note, tick "Sick note issued" and complete:

FieldNotes
Number of daysDuration of the sick note period.
Start dateFirst day of the sick leave period.
EmployerName of the patient's employer (for the sick note document).
Lab and Radiology Requests

In the consultation form, enter any lab or radiology requests in the dedicated fields — one request per line. For example:

💡
Lab requests: FBC, U&E, LFT, HbA1c, TSH
Radiology requests: Chest X-ray (PA view), Abdominal ultrasound
Printing a Consultation Note

Open any consultation and click the Print button. A clean, printable version opens which you can print or save as PDF for medico-legal records.

Finding an Old Consultation

Go to Consultations in the sidebar and use the search bar to search by patient name or keywords in the assessment field. You can also open a patient's profile and click the Consultations tab to see that patient's history only.

Chapter 5
Documents & Prescriptions

The Documents module stores prescriptions, sick notes, referral letters, and any other clinical documents linked to a patient. Documents can be created in the system, printed, and optionally attached as files.

Adding a New Document
  1. 1
    Click Documents in the sidebar, then "+ New Document".
  2. 2
    Select the patient.
  3. 3
    Choose the document type (Prescription, Sick Note, Referral Letter, or Other).
  4. 4
    Select or create a category to keep documents organised (e.g. "Chronic medication", "Acute illness").
  5. 5
    Add notes or content in the Notes field.
  6. 6
    Optionally attach a file (a PDF, image, or scanned document).
  7. 7
    Click "Save Document".
Searching Documents

On the Documents page, use the search bar to find documents by patient name, document type, or category.

Printing a Document

Open any document and click Print. The print view includes your practice name, address, and registration number at the top — making it suitable for handing directly to the patient or posting.

💡
To save as PDF: Click Print, then in the print dialog choose "Save as PDF" as the printer destination. This gives you a digitally stored copy.
Chapter 6
Tasks & Reminders

Tasks are your personal follow-up list — lab results to chase, callbacks to make, referral outcomes to check. They appear on your Dashboard and can be linked to a specific patient.

Adding a Task
  1. 1
    Click Tasks in the sidebar.
  2. 2
    Click "+ Add Task".
  3. 3
    Enter a clear title (e.g. "Chase FBC result — John Smith").
  4. 4
    Set the due date and priority.
  5. 5
    Optionally link the task to a patient.
  6. 6
    Click "Save Task".
Task Priorities
PriorityUse for
HighUrgent clinical follow-ups — abnormal results, same-day callbacks, deteriorating patients.
MediumRoutine follow-ups — standard lab results, prescription renewals, referral acknowledgements.
LowAdministrative or non-time-sensitive items.

Tasks are sorted with High priority at the top, then by due date. Overdue tasks remain visible until marked done.

Completing a Task

Click the checkbox next to any task — on the Tasks page or directly on the Dashboard — to mark it as done. Completed tasks move to the "Completed" section at the bottom of the Tasks page for your records.

Follow-up Reminders from Consultations

When you set a Follow-up Date in a consultation note, that patient automatically appears in the "Follow-ups Due" panel on your Dashboard on and after that date — no manual task creation needed.

Chapter 7
Billing & Invoicing

The Billing module handles private invoicing for your practice. Invoices are auto-numbered, support multiple line items, calculate VAT at 15%, and can be emailed directly to patients or printed for hand delivery.

Creating an Invoice
  1. 1
    Click Billing in the sidebar, then "+ New Invoice".
  2. 2
    Select the patient. The invoice number is generated automatically (format: INV-YYYYMM-001).
  3. 3
    The issue date defaults to today, and the due date defaults to 30 days from today. Adjust if needed.
  4. 4
    Add line items — each with a description, quantity, unit price, and optional procedure code.
  5. 5
    Optionally fill in ICD-10 code, procedure codes, and medical aid authorisation number.
  6. 6
    Click "Save Invoice". The invoice is saved as a Draft.
Invoice Statuses
StatusMeaning
DraftCreated but not yet sent. You can still edit all fields.
SentEmailed or handed to the patient. The system records the date sent.
PaidPayment received. Click "Mark as Paid" to update the status and record the amount received.
OverdueAutomatically flagged when a Sent or Draft invoice passes its due date without payment.
CancelledInvoice voided. Use this instead of deleting to maintain a complete billing history.
Sending an Invoice by Email
  1. 1
    Open the invoice.
  2. 2
    Click "Send Email". The email is sent to the address on the patient's record.
  3. 3
    The invoice status automatically changes to Sent.
⚠
Email requires a patient email address. If the patient has no email address on file, update their record before sending. See Chapter 2 — Patient Records.
Recording a Payment
  1. 1
    Open the invoice.
  2. 2
    Click "Mark as Paid".
  3. 3
    Enter the amount received and the receipt number.
  4. 4
    The status changes to Paid and the balance due updates to zero.
Printing an Invoice

Open any invoice and click Print. The printed invoice includes your practice name, address, HPCSA registration number, all line items, VAT calculation, and balance due.

VAT on Invoices

VAT is calculated at 15% on the subtotal of all line items. The invoice shows the subtotal, VAT amount, and total separately. If your practice is not VAT-registered, do not use the VAT line — contact your administrator to adjust the setting.

Chapter 8
Analytics

The Analytics page gives you a bird's-eye view of your practice's performance and patient population. It is read-only — no data entry is required. The figures update automatically as you use the system.

What the Analytics Page Shows
SectionWhat it tells you
14-Day Appointment TrendA bar graph showing how many appointments you had each day over the last two weeks. Useful for spotting busy periods and quiet spells.
Appointment Status BreakdownHow your appointments are split between Scheduled, Completed, Cancelled, and No-Show as percentages. Tracks your DNA (Did Not Attend) rate.
Top Chronic ConditionsThe six most common chronic conditions across your entire patient base. Useful for understanding your practice's clinical burden.
Patient Summary TotalsTotal patients, total appointments, total consultations, and total documents on the system.
Patient DemographicsHow many patients have medical aid, how many have documented allergies or chronic conditions, and the gender split.
How to Use Analytics in Practice
📈 Staffing
  • Use the 14-day trend to identify your busiest days
  • Plan locum cover around recurring peaks
🏥 Clinical audit
  • Review top chronic conditions to inform health promotion activities
  • Monitor your Completed vs No-Show rate
💰 Revenue planning
  • Track total consultations to estimate monthly income
  • Monitor medical aid coverage in your patient base
📋 POPIA compliance
  • Patient demographics help verify consent records are complete
  • Use totals to confirm all active patients are on the system
Chapter 9
Quick Reference

A single-page summary for daily use. Print this page and keep it at your desk until the system becomes second nature.

Daily Workflow — Typical Consulting Day
  1. 1
    Morning: Check the Dashboard — review today's appointment list, check for overdue tasks, and note any follow-ups due.
  2. 2
    Each patient: Open their record → record vitals → complete the consultation note (SOAP) → issue documents or prescriptions if needed → update the appointment status to "Completed".
  3. 3
    After consulting: Create any invoices for today's patients. Send or print as appropriate.
  4. 4
    End of day: Tick off completed tasks. Add any new tasks for tomorrow (pending results, callbacks).

ProblemWhat to do
