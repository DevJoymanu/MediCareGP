Chapter 11
Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions GPs ask most often when starting with G.P CRM.

Patients
Can two patients share the same name?
Yes. Each patient receives a unique file number (e.g. GP-0047) on registration, so two patients called "John Smith" are distinguished by their file number, ID number, and date of birth — not their name.
What if a patient does not have a South African ID number?
Select Passport as the ID type and enter the passport number instead. The ID field is optional — you can register a patient with name, date of birth, and phone number only if no ID document is available.
Can I merge two duplicate patient records?
Not automatically. If you accidentally created a duplicate, manually copy any important clinical notes into the correct record, then delete the duplicate. Contact your administrator if you need help identifying which record has the full history.
How do I record multiple allergies or chronic conditions?
Enter each allergy or chronic condition on a separate line in the respective field when registering or editing the patient. For example:

Penicillin
Sulfonamides
Aspirin
Can I upload a scanned ID or consent form to a patient's record?
Yes. Go to Documents → New Document, select the patient, choose type "Other", and use the file attachment field to upload a scanned PDF or image. It will appear under the Documents tab on their profile.
Is patient data backed up?
Yes. The system runs on a managed cloud database (Railway PostgreSQL) which is automatically backed up. Your data is not stored on your local computer — it is safe even if your device is lost or damaged.
Appointments
Can I book more than one appointment at the same time?
The system does not prevent double-booking — it is your responsibility to check the schedule before booking. Filter the appointment list by date and time to confirm a slot is free before adding a new booking.
How do I mark a patient as "seen" after their appointment?
Open the appointment and change the status from Scheduled to Completed. Do this after you have finished the consultation. Alternatively, write a Consultation Note and update the status at the same time.
Can I see a week or month view of appointments?
The list view can be filtered by a date range — set a start and end date to see a full week or month. A calendar grid view is not currently available, but the filtered list shows the same information in date-time order.
What happens to cancelled appointments — are they deleted?
No. Cancelled appointments remain in the system with a Cancelled status. This is intentional for medico-legal record-keeping. You can filter them out of your view using the status filter.
Consultation Notes
Can I edit a consultation note after saving it?
Yes. Open the consultation and click Edit. There is no time lock — you can amend notes at any time. For medico-legal best practice, avoid editing notes after the fact unless correcting a genuine error, and note the date of any amendment in the Plan field.
Does the system generate a printed sick note with my practice details?
Yes. When you tick "Sick note issued" in the consultation form and fill in the number of days, start date, and employer, a print-ready sick note is available from the consultation's Print view. It includes your practice name, address, and HPCSA registration number automatically.
Can I search through my old SOAP notes?
Yes. Go to Consultations and use the search bar to search by patient name or by keywords in the assessment field. For example, searching "hypertension" will surface all consultations where that word appears in the assessment. You can also filter by going to a specific patient's profile and clicking the Consultations tab.
Can I link a consultation to an appointment?
Yes. When creating a new consultation, there is an optional "Link to Appointment" dropdown. Linking them is recommended as it helps you track which appointments resulted in documented consultations, and avoids confusion with unattended slots.
Is there a template for common consultation types (e.g. hypertension review)?
Not currently — each SOAP note starts blank. A practical workaround is to keep a text file with your standard consultation templates and paste the relevant sections into the fields. This significantly speeds up note-writing for chronic disease follow-ups.
Billing & Invoicing
Can I edit an invoice after sending it?
Yes, but exercise caution. Editing a Sent invoice changes the figures the patient has already received. Best practice is to cancel the original invoice, create a corrected one, and re-send it — noting "Replaces INV-XXXXXX" in the notes field.
The patient paid less than the full amount — how do I record a partial payment?
Click Mark as Paid on the invoice and enter the actual amount received. The invoice will show both the total and the amount paid, and calculate the outstanding balance automatically. The status will update to Paid regardless — you may want to keep it as Sent until the balance is cleared.
How do I issue an invoice to the patient's medical aid instead of the patient directly?
Fill in the ICD-10 code, procedure codes, and authorisation number fields on the invoice. These fields are specifically designed for medical aid billing. Print the invoice and submit it to the scheme directly — the system does not currently submit claims electronically to medical aids.
My practice is not VAT-registered. Can I turn off VAT on invoices?
VAT is calculated at 15% by default. If you are not VAT-registered, contact your administrator to adjust the VAT_RATE setting to 0 in the system settings. This will remove VAT from all invoices going forward.
Can I set my own invoice numbering format?
Invoice numbers are auto-generated in the format INV-YYYYMM-### (e.g. INV-202605-001). This format cannot be changed through the interface. If you need a custom format, contact your administrator.
What email address do invoices get sent from?
Invoices are sent from the email address configured in the practice settings — by default this is the practice Gmail account set up during installation. The patient's name and invoice details appear in the email body. Check with your administrator if you are unsure which address is being used.
Data, Security & Privacy
Who can see patient records?
Only users with a login account can access the system. The system does not currently support role-based access (e.g. receptionist vs doctor views) — any logged-in user can see all records. Each person should have their own login; never share accounts. Contact your administrator to create additional user accounts.
Can a patient request access to their records under POPIA?
Yes. Under POPIA, patients have the right to access their personal information. You can print or export their consultation notes, invoices, and documents from the system and provide these directly. Ensure you verify the patient's identity before releasing any records.
What happens to the data if we stop using the system?
Your data is stored in the cloud database. Before cancelling the service, export all patient records (consult your administrator for a data export). Under POPIA, you are required to retain medical records for a minimum period — ensure you have a compliant offline backup before decommissioning the system.
Is the system secure? Can someone hack into it?
The system uses HTTPS encryption for all data in transit, and the database is not publicly accessible. Your main security responsibilities are: use a strong password, do not share your login, log out on shared computers, and do not access the system on public Wi-Fi without a VPN.
Technical & General
Which browsers work best?
The system works in all modern browsers. Google Chrome and Microsoft Edge are recommended — they offer the best print-to-PDF support for consultation notes and invoices. Avoid Internet Explorer.
Can I use the system on a tablet or smartphone?
Yes. The system is accessible from any device with a browser. On a tablet (e.g. iPad), it works well for reading records and writing notes. On a smartphone screen, some tables may require horizontal scrolling — a tablet or laptop is recommended for intensive data entry.
What happens if the internet goes down during a consultation?
Any unsaved data will be lost if the connection drops before you click Save. As a precaution, click Save frequently while writing long consultation notes — treat it like a word processor. Keep a paper backup of critical information for network-down scenarios.
Can multiple staff members use the system at the same time?
Yes. The system supports multiple simultaneous users. Reception can be booking appointments while you write consultation notes, for example. Each person should log in with their own account.
Can I add my practice logo to printed documents?
Not through the interface currently — printed documents display your practice name, address, phone number, and HPCSA registration number as configured in the system settings. Logo support would require a system update by your administrator.
How do I request a new feature or report a problem?
Contact your practice administrator with a description of the feature you need or the problem you encountered, including the page you were on and what you were trying to do. Screenshots are very helpful for bug reports.
